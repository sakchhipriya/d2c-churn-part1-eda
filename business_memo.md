# Business Memo

## Subject: Customer Churn Risk Assessment and Retention Opportunities

### Executive Summary

An exploratory analysis was conducted to understand customer behavior and identify potential churn drivers. The analysis included customer demographics, purchasing behavior, spending patterns, support interactions, web activity, campaign engagement, and churn outcomes.

### Key Findings

1. Customers with lower web engagement exhibited higher churn tendencies.
2. Lower campaign engagement was associated with increased churn risk.
3. Product return behavior may indicate customer dissatisfaction and future churn.
4. Loyalty-program participation appears to support customer retention.
5. Support-ticket volume alone was not a strong indicator of churn.

### Recommended Actions

#### Increase Customer Engagement

Develop personalized campaigns for customers showing declining web activity.

#### Strengthen Loyalty Programs

Encourage customers to progress through loyalty tiers using targeted incentives.

#### Monitor Return Activity

Use return behavior as an early-warning signal for potential churn.

#### Improve Campaign Targeting

Focus marketing efforts on customers with low engagement levels.

#### Protect High-Value Customers

Prioritize retention initiatives for customers contributing significant revenue.

### Conclusion

The analysis suggests that engagement-related metrics provide valuable indicators of future churn. These findings should guide retention strategy development and future predictive modeling efforts.
